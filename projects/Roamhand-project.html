Abstract
Dexterous manipulation has been one of the top research fields in robotics. We have seen incredible work on robotic pick-and-place, but most of the related tasks are performed with a parallel gripper or suction gripper. The low dexterity of such end effectors limit the complexity of the tasks. In order to achieve general purposes of robotics, dexterous manipulation is essential. In this project, we study the dexterous manipulations on different tasks with a 4-digit robotic hand kinematics. The tasks include rotating objects by finger-gaiting, transferring grasping poses from pinching to enveloping, and flipping objects extrinsically on a tabletop. The goal is to synthesize and optimize the kinematics design to allow general dexterous manipulation.
