DeCoR: Learning New Sound Features with Delayed Codebook Regularizer
Columbia University, Course Project, ELEN 4810 Digital Signal Processing
Sept. 2022 - Dec. 2022
Abstract
In this work, we incrementally update an audio feature extractor incorporating arrival sounds. Naively training the model on new data of new classes results in catastrophic forgetting of the previously learned features. To overcome this problem, we present Delayed Codebook Regularizer (DeCoR), a compute- and memory-efficient method to preserve model knowledge on previous data. We construct feature codebooks when the model enters a new task and use these early feature codes to regularize afterward training. Experiments show that our method improves audio classification accuracy with only a marginal overhead on resources.
Comparison of Continual Learning Methods. We claim that our DeCoR is more compute and memory efficient than the other three. Replay requires extra storage for the audio rehearsal and computation to train on the rehearsal; Knowledge distillation requires additional storage for the past model checkpoint and computation to forward the teacher; Contrastive learning demands at least two augmentations of the same audio and thus at least twice amount of computation. Our DeCoR only stores feature codes and lookups, which are smaller than model checkpoints or unencoded waveforms, and do knowledge distillation from existing codes, which is faster than repeatedly forwarding the model.
Alternative Optimization of DeCoR. Codebook $\mathcal{C}_t$ is updated at the start of task $\mathcal{T}_t$ by INCREMENT; Model $\mathcal{M}_t$ is updated throughout $\mathcal{T}_t$ by DISTILL. There is at most one-task delay of the knowledge encoded by the model and the codebook. Multiple codebooks and models are drawn in the figure for clarity. In reality, only the last codebook and model are used.
