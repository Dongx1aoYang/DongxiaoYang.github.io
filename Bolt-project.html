Bolt Tightening Robot for Power Transmission Tower
Shanghai Jiao Tong University, State Grid China, Research, Capstone Design
Sept. 2021 - Aug. 2022
Abstract
Strengthening the routine maintenance of power grid has emerged as a critical issue with Chinese power system's ongoing expansion. High-voltage transmission towers must be manually climbed in the traditional maintenance method, but this operation has a number of hazardous, difficult, and burdensome issues that are influenced by the environment outside. In order to overcome obstacles like climbing nails on high-voltage transmission towers, this paper proposes a magnetically three-legged alternating mobile bolt tightening robot mechanism, which consists of a communication control unit, a bolt tightening mechanism, and a robot motion mechanism that can move alternately and work in concert. The bolt location precision and robot work efficiency are improved by the vision camera mounted on the bolt tightening module, the electromagnetic suction cup ensures the robot won't fall off the tower, and the control system enables remote robot operation monitoring. Using the aforementioned technique, the high-voltage transmission tower bolt fastening robot's 3D model and static analysis model were created, and the precise structure and motion parameters were provided. The experiment demonstrate that, in the condition of unmanned towers climbing, the robot can successfully complete the identification and placement, alignment control, and fastening of bolts with efficiency and accuracy, which enhances the effectiveness and safety of bolt fastening in high-voltage towers. Mechanical design overview with dimensions (control units are not displayed).
Gait pattern of the robot. The central leg and side legs alternately lift and advance. Climbing downward is done in the opposite order. The slide modules' stroke lengths have been precisely calibrated to allow the foot to pass over the nails without risk, and the moving pattern is adaptable enough to any place along the path. All three legs adhere to the surface with all electromagnets turned on when the robot tightens the bolt.
Overview of the robot system. The motion control is based on Raspberry Pi. The protocol used to send and receive commands and video is TCP/IP in Python.
